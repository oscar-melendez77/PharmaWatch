The Problem

Every drug's risk is buried across a dozen datasets.

When you're prescribed a medication, the signals that actually tell you how risky it is for you live in separate silos: the FDA's raw adverse-event reports, decades of PubMed research, structured drug labels, and thousands of real patient conversations. None of them talk to each other, and none of them are tuned to your age, sex, or habits.

PharmaWatch joins all four into a single per-drug profile, trains four gradient-boosted models to score serious reactions, hospitalization, death, and disability, and layers a transparent personalization pass on top — then lets you interrogate the result in plain language. Everything below runs from one pipeline.

The Data

Four Sources. One Profile.

Each source captures a different slice of drug risk — regulatory signal, peer-reviewed evidence, official labelling, and lived patient experience. All public, all reproducible.

The Architecture

Raw APIs → Lakehouse → Models → Agent.

A multi-cloud lakehouse pipeline: Kafka + Airflow ingestion, a Great Expectations quality gate, Apache Iceberg on S3, Scala Spark transforms on Databricks, a switchable Snowflake/BigQuery warehouse, dbt marts, LightGBM + MLflow, and a ChromaDB-backed RAG agent — served through FastAPI.
